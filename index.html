Podcast 2025-11-20

Looking for a turnaround in transmission

The podcast discusses the challenges and importance of expanding the US transmission grid to support clean energy, data centers, and AI industry growth. Key points include the slow progress in building new transmission lines, the impact of federal policies like FERC Order 1920, and the need for federal leadership in infrastructure development. Recent developments, such as SPP's approval for a 765 KV transmission backbone and the cancellation of a loan guarantee for the Greenbelt Express project, are also analyzed. The episode emphasizes the critical role of transmission infrastructure in meeting increasing electricity demand and enabling the transition to a cleaner and more reliable energy system.

Podcast 2025-11-18

New Mexico proves America can still build [partner content]

Rob Black and Jim Kenny from the New Mexico Economic Development Department and Environment Department discuss how they are streamlining government processes to attract clean energy projects. New Mexico's white glove service provides personalized assistance for permits and investments. They emphasize balancing economic development with environmental protection, showcasing their focus on clean energy innovation, including fusion, geothermal, and deep tech. By offering incentives like tax credits and low-interest loans, along with a $65 billion sovereign wealth fund investing in energy innovation, New Mexico aims to become a hub for the fusion industry and a leader in clean energy by the end of the decade.

Podcast 2025-11-13

Driving down the cost of green hydrogen

The podcast discusses the challenges and developments in the green hydrogen market, focusing on the burst of the hype bubble surrounding green hydrogen due to high costs and unmet expectations. The CEO of Electric Hydrogen, Raffi Garibedian, shares insights on the historical context of the market, cost breakdown of traditional electrolyzer projects, and solutions to reduce capital costs. Electric Hydrogen's approach emphasizes system-level thinking, modularization, and dense technology to minimize EPC costs and overall system expenses. The transcript also mentions the importance of understanding non-cost barriers in clean energy deployment and highlights the AI industry's increasing demand for reliable, scalable power solutions like Bloom Energy and Energy Hub.

Podcast 2025-11-06

Inside a $300 million bet on AI for physical R&D

The podcast discusses the advancements in AI for materials discovery, particularly focusing on Periodic Labs, a company working on AI for high-temperature superconductors. The conversation delves into the challenges of AI in making breakthrough discoveries versus incremental progress, highlighting the importance of combining digital and physical domains to accelerate discoveries. The podcast also touches on the significance of superconductors, their potential impact, and the various improvements needed to achieve breakthroughs in the field. Additionally, it explores the role of AI, high-throughput experiments, and automated processes in advancing material science. The episode concludes by addressing the rewards for AI systems in achieving goals like finding room temperature superconductors and the complexities of setting such objectives.
