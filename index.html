The gas turbine crunch

The podcast discusses the current dynamics of the gas turbine market, focusing on timelines, pricing, and market drivers. The industry has evolved over the decades with key players like Mitsubishi, Siemens, and GE Vrenova dominating. Lead times for gas turbines are around 36 to 48 months, with efforts to improve by OEMs to avoid upsetting customers. Prices have increased by around 30-35% in the last five years due to factors like raw material costs and demand. Market drivers include grid-scale battery storage, coal plant retirements, renewable energy expansion, data center development, and natural gas availability. The market is segmented based on size, with different dynamics for small, medium, and jumbo-sized units.

How geothermal gets built

Latitude Media is hosting the Transition AI conference in 2025 focusing on energy infrastructure in the AI-driven load growth era.
