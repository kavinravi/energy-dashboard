Can AI revolutionize EPC?

The podcast discusses challenges in engineering procurement construction (EPC) projects, focusing on misaligned incentives leading to cost overruns and delays. The guest, Alex Modan, CEO of Unlimited Industries, aims to improve EPC processes using modern technology and iterative design to reduce uncertainties and optimize project designs. Additionally, the podcast highlights Energy Hub's role in transforming everyday devices into flexible energy resources for utilities, and Third Way's study on non-cost barriers hindering clean energy projects. The conversation delves into the complexities of EPC projects, the need for continuous improvement, and the potential for leveraging engineering to enhance cost certainty and efficiency in project delivery.

Podcast 2025-12-04

Who benefits from the AI power bottleneck?

The podcast discusses the importance of monitoring incentives in the context of power scarcity affecting AI growth. Hyperscalers, like Amazon and Microsoft, are at the center of data center expansion and have incentives to highlight power constraints to drive infrastructure development. OEMs and service providers supplying data center equipment also benefit from promoting the power scarcity narrative to boost demand for their services. The conversation delves into the complexities of these incentives and the strategic decisions these players make in response to power challenges. Energy companies like Energy Hub and Bloom Energy offer solutions to address surging electricity demand and power AI operations efficiently.

Podcast 2025-11-20

Looking for a turnaround in transmission

The podcast discusses the challenges and importance of building new high-voltage transmission lines in the US to meet the surging electricity demand, particularly from data centers and AI industries. Despite recent upticks in transmission projects, many are long overdue and face regulatory and funding hurdles. The conversation highlights the need for federal leadership and bipartisan support to expedite transmission infrastructure development. Additionally, innovative solutions from companies like Energy Hub and Bloom Energy are helping utilities and data centers manage energy demand efficiently and sustainably. The episode underscores the critical role of transmission expansion in supporting clean energy deployment and economic growth.

Podcast 2025-11-18

New Mexico proves America can still build [partner content]

In this podcast transcript, Rob Black and Jim Kenny from New Mexico discuss their approach to making government work for the clean energy economy. They emphasize the importance of avoiding the traditional trade-off between economic growth and environmental protection. New Mexico's strategy involves providing white glove service, regulatory certainty, and quick permitting processes to attract clean energy projects. They highlight initiatives such as the state's sovereign wealth fund investing in deep tech and energy innovation, focusing on fusion, geothermal, and carbon capture technologies. By combining financial incentives, regulatory support, and research partnerships, New Mexico aims to become a hub for the fusion industry and drive economic growth while addressing climate challenges.
