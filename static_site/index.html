Podcast 2025-06-19

The story of steam

The podcast "Catalyst" delves into the energy transition, focusing on waste heat and the decarbonization of industrial steam. Steam is a crucial component in various industries, with half of global industrial energy use dedicated to generating steam. Traditional methods involve burning fossil fuels, primarily natural gas, to produce steam. Options for decarbonization include electrifying boilers using resistance or heat pump technology. While resistance boilers are the most common, heat pumps offer a more efficient and sustainable solution. However, challenges like the high cost and complexity of capturing and utilizing waste heat hinder widespread adoption. The discussion emphasizes the need for innovative, standardized approaches to decarbonize industrial steam production effectively.

The state of play of data center development

The podcast covers the latest trends in the energy transition, focusing on data centers and the energy storage sector. It delves into the importance of proximity, power availability, and infrastructure planning in data center development, particularly in regions like Northern Virginia. The discussion includes insights on workload requirements, power constraints, lead times for critical components like transformers, and the evolving scale of data centers to meet diverse customer needs. Additionally, it highlights the role of advanced analytics platforms like ANSA in aiding developers in making informed decisions.

Podcast 2025-06-05

The gas turbine crunch

The podcast discusses the current state and dynamics of the gas turbine market, focusing on the lead times, pricing, market drivers, and supply chain complexities. The conversation delves into historical boom and bust cycles, the impact of factors like raw material prices, aerospace industry demand, and oil and gas market trends on gas turbine production and pricing. Key points include OEM efforts to manage lead times, market drivers influencing various turbine sizes, and the evolving landscape of power generation and energy storage. The discussion highlights the interplay between different industry sectors and the cautious optimism surrounding future market developments.

How geothermal gets built

Latitude Media is hosting a Transition AI conference in 2025 focusing on AI-driven load growth in energy infrastructure. The podcast discusses geothermal power's resurgence, highlighting conventional hydrothermal fields and enhanced geothermal systems (EGS). Carl Huyland, CEO of Zansgar, explains the history and challenges of geothermal exploration, emphasizing the need for better technology to locate and develop resources effectively. The process involves drilling temperature gradient holes, assessing data interpretation, confirming resource potential, and establishing a conceptual model before progressing to drill production wells. The conversation delves into the technical aspects and complexities involved in geothermal exploration and development. Energy companies like ANSA and Energy Hub are also highlighted for their contributions to energy storage and grid flexibility.
